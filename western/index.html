About the Program





Psychology is the study of people's performance, behaviour, and mental operations. People who are intrested in psychology study human behaviours through different fields, including biology, thinking processes and social experiences etc. Concepts and theories of psychology are important for us to understand ourselves. They are applied in both labotarories and the context of all aspects of our daily lives.















A psychology degree prepares students for various potential careers across different psychology-related fields, for example, couselling, human resources and training specialists. Furtuhur education including Master and Doctor is always required.

Courses

Psych 1000 - Introduction to Psychology

Description: An introductory survey of the methods and findings of modern scientific psychology. The basic and general introduction to psychology field.

Topics: The topics are history and methodology, biological psychology, sensation and perception, learning and motivation, verbal and cognitive processes, developmental psychology, social psychology, individual differences (intelligence and personality), and clinical psychology.

Opinion: If you are interested in psychology and would like to learn more about it, the course is just right for you! The lectures are structured to cover different fields of psychology (e.g., Social Psychology, Cognitive Psychology and I/O Psychology). Students are able to learn psychology as a science while getting the opportunity to find the fields that they are mostly interesed in! Personally, the fields that I enjoyed most were biopsychology and social psychology.






Psych 2135 - Cognitive Psychology

Description: An introduction to empirical, computational, and theoretical approaches to the study of human cognitive processes.

Topics: The course will show how these diverse psychological processes are related to and influence one another. The topics surveyed will include: perception, attention, memory, concepts, language and problem solving.

Opinion: Cognitive psychology is one of my favourite psychology course. As a foundation of psychology, this course helped me understanding how our behaviours processed with regards to minds. It also prepares for students for higher level related courses such as Research Methods for Cognitive Psychology and Psychology of Thinking. The professor also made the difficult stuff interesting and understandable.








Psych 2800E - Research Methods in Psychology

Description: Using selected examples, this course will introduce students to the variety of ways to conduct research in psychology. Also the students are required to practice conducting owns studies.

Topics: Topics to be covered include: the scientific approach; ethical issues in human and animal research; designing, running, analyzing and writing up a research project; experimental, observational and correlational research strategies.

Opinion: the course is composed of lecture and the labs.This course introduced basic research methods of psychology studies, including the ethical principles, the types, validity and reliability etc. I don't think it is an easy course, but the experience of conducting own psyhcology experiment is very fun and valuable.








Psych 3371F - Therapeutic Counselling

Description: This couse will introduce students to the field of professional counselling and psychotherapy.

Topics: various processes and techniques of therapeutic counselling; special relationship problems; transference, resistance; interpretation techniques and group counselling; special areas of application of psychotherapeutic counselling: marriage, family, human relations in education and in industry, counselling and problems of values.

Opinion: This is definitely one of my favourite courses! Different from other psychology courses that have an emphasis on research, this course is more related to our daily lives. Students will benefit from the course in terms of counselling knowledge as well as the relationships with people around and theirselves.
